I am a joint Ph.D. candidate in [Public Policy](https://sanford.duke.edu/) and [Sociology](https://sociology.duke.edu/) at [Duke University](https://scholars.duke.edu/person/shuyi.qiu), where I am affiliated with the [Duke Population Research Institute (DUPRI)](https://dupri.duke.edu/) and the [Duke Center for Child and Family Policy (CCFP)](https://childandfamilypolicy.duke.edu/). I am also a doctoral fellow affiliate at the [Max Planck Institute for Demographic Research (MPIDR)](https://www.imprs-phds.mpg.de/). <br> 

My research primarily examine **how inequalities in wealth and health are produced and reproduced through family process or broader structural constructs, and how public policies may mitigate or exacerbate these dynamics.** I am particularly interested in quantitatively analyzing these processes from life-course (temporal) and socio-ecological (spatial) perspectives. [My work](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=UXMKdLcAAAAJ&hl=en-us) has been published or is forthcoming in journals like *International Journal of Epidemiology*, *Demography*, *Social Currents*, and etc. You can find more about my research and interests in this [Q&A](https://sanford.duke.edu/story/phd-student-qa-shuyi-qiu/). <br>

Currently, my curiosities have been drawn to two broad topics: <br>
 (1) inequality in young adults’ wealth accumulation <br>
 (2) the consequences of living in places with high levels of wealth inequality <br>
