# Open-Source Tools for Data Processing
To answer these questions, I primarily rely on quantitative methods with large-scale longitudinal and population-based survey data, including the [Panel Study of Income Dynamics (PSID)](https://psidonline.isr.umich.edu/), [General Social Survey (GSS)](https://gss.norc.org/) and [micro-level Decennial Census data](https://usa.ipums.org/usa/) in the United States, as well as the [China Family Panel Studies (CFPS)](https://www.isss.pku.edu.cn/cfps/en/index.htm) and [China Kadoorie Biobank (CKB)](https://www.ckbiobank.org/) in the context of China. I also passionate about developing open-source tools that facilitate processing these complex dataset. You can find some of my ongoing projects and tools I am maintaining on my [GitHub](https://github.com/Qcrates) page.<br>
